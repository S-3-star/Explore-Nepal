History of Nepal

Nepal has a rich and ancient history dating back thousands of years. The Kathmandu Valley was first ruled by the Kirat dynasty, known for agriculture and early governance. The Licchavi dynasty (400–750 AD) brought a golden age of art, architecture, and administration. The Malla dynasty (1200–1768 AD) promoted festivals, music, and Durbar Square architecture. In the 18th century, King Prithvi Narayan Shah unified small kingdoms to form modern Nepal. Today, Nepal preserves its historical traditions while embracing modernization, tourism, and adventure.

About Nepal

Nepal, nestled in the heart of the Himalayas, is a land of majestic mountains, vibrant culture, and rich traditions. From Mount Everest to the serene lakes of Pokhara, the country offers breathtaking natural beauty. Nepal is also a spiritual hub, home to Lumbini, the birthplace of Lord Buddha, and countless temples and monasteries. The Nepali people, known for their warmth and hospitality, celebrate numerous festivals and maintain customs that have lasted centuries. Today, Nepal combines its historical heritage with a growing tourism and adventure industry, inviting visitors to explore its unique landscapes, culture, and traditions.
