About Nepal

Nepal is a South Asian country located in the Himalayas, bordered by India and China. It's known for Mount Everest, the highest peak in the world, and Lumbini, the birthplace of Lord Buddha. Tourism, agriculture, and culture are at the heart of Nepalese life. Explore the majestic mountains, rich spiritual heritage, and pristine natural landscapes that make Nepal unique.

History of Nepal

Nepal possesses a rich and ancient history that dates back thousands of years. Early civilizations flourished in the Kathmandu Valley, where the Kirat dynasty ruled before the rise of the Licchavi dynasty. The Licchavi period is considered a golden era marked by advancements in art, architecture, trade, and governance.

Following this period, the Malla dynasty contributed significantly to Nepal’s cultural and architectural heritage. Magnificent temples, palaces, and public squares built during this time still stand today in Kathmandu, Patan, and Bhaktapur, reflecting the artistic excellence of medieval Nepal.

In the 18th century, King Prithvi Narayan Shah of Gorkha initiated the unification campaign that brought together many small kingdoms into a single nation, forming modern Nepal. His vision helped preserve Nepal’s independence during a time of expanding foreign influence in South Asia.

Nepal later experienced the Rana regime, followed by political reforms that introduced democracy. Today, Nepal is a federal democratic republic that proudly maintains its historical traditions while progressing toward modernization and development.
